Executive Summary

PEARS III is the recompete of the FAA's primary aviation environmental policy and engineering analysis contract. The work covers NEPA environmental review, noise modeling (AEDT), air quality analysis, climate/emissions policy (CORSIA), NAS simulation modeling, software development for environmental tools, and policy research supporting FAA's Office of Environment & Energy (AEE). This is a Total Small Business Set-Aside under NAICS 541330 (NEPA exception, $47M size standard).

Contract lineage: PEARS I (DTFAWA-10-R-00038, ATAC, ~2010) → PEARS II (693KA918D00005, JD Rovolus LLC, 2018; plus DTFAWA16D00035, ATAC, 2016) → PEARS III (693KA9-25-R-00016, TBD). Two incumbents have split this work across generations.

Status: The FAA posted Q&A responses to the Draft SIR tonight (March 11, 2026). The final SIR is expected to incorporate significant revisions based on 80 vendor questions — including revised LCATs, pricing templates, past performance requirements, and SOW clarifications. No proposal due date yet — this is still in Draft SIR phase.

II. Scope — 17 SOW Elements

What PEARS III Covers

The contract supports the FAA's Office of Environment & Energy (AEE) across 17 SOW elements (C.3.1 through C.3.17). Based on the Q&A, the major work areas include:

Environmental Policy & NEPA

  • FAA Order 1050.1G — NEPA environmental review for aviation projects (corrected from 1050.1F in Q&A #1)
  • CORSIA — Carbon Offsetting and Reduction Scheme for International Aviation implementation support
  • Environmental impact assessments, policy analysis, regulatory support

Modeling & Simulation (C.3.4)

  • AEDT (Aviation Environmental Design Tool) — primary noise/emissions modeling
  • ANOPP, AAM, RUMBLE, NOISEMAP — noise modeling tools
  • TAAM, ACM, Runway Simulator, ADSIM+, SIMMOD — airspace/airport simulation
  • SWAC, SIMCORE, NASPAC — NAS-wide simulation capabilities
  • ASPM, ETMS, TFMS, PDARS — operational data systems
  • GIS — geographic information systems analysis
  • Database design for operational, environmental, and economic data (cloud or on-premise per TO)

Software Development (C.3.11)

  • Requirements analysis, software design, development for environmental/operational/economic modeling
  • AI/ML, synthetic data, digital twins — FAA "exploring" but specifics will be per task order
  • Full SDLC — could be cloud (AWS) or legacy depending on TO requirements

Other Key Areas

  • Air quality analysis and monitoring
  • Climate science and emissions research
  • Economic analysis (cost-benefit, regulatory impact)
  • Training and knowledge transfer
  • Technical writing and documentation
Important: Tools Are Not Prescriptive

FAA confirmed in Q&A #3, #4, #43: "Tools listed are examples and not intended to be an all-inclusive list." Specific requirements will be "project dependent and specified in individual Task Order requirements." This opens the door to proposing modern tools alongside legacy ones.

IV. Key Q&A Signals

What the 80 Questions Reveal

Total Small Business Set-Aside — Confirmed

Q&A #21: "The SAM posting provides this information. It will be a Total Small Business Set-Aside." This is the single most important signal. Only small businesses (under $47M annual revenue) can bid as prime. Large firms like SAIC, Booz Allen, and Leidos are out. JVs allowed if both venturers are individually small (Q&A #50).

Past Performance — Major Revisions Coming

Significant Changes to Past Performance Requirements
  • Reduced from 4 to 3 examples (Q&A #22) — responds to industry concern about small business burden
  • Subcontractor experience now accepted — "no more than one may be performed by a subcontractor" (Q&A #22)
  • Federal-only requirement maintained — no commercial or state/local allowed (Q&A #22, #24, #38)
  • 36-month recency window holds — FAA rejected 60-month requests (Q&A #23)
  • "Very Relevant" definition unchanged — "essentially the same scope and magnitude" stands despite protest concerns (Q&A #10)

Pricing — Simplified

  • Indirect rates removed — Tab L-1C indirect rate requirements deleted entirely (Q&A #15, #20)
  • No estimated hours will be provided — FAA cost model stays internal (Q&A #8, #12, #19)
  • Contractor AND Government site rates required for all LCATs (Q&A #14, #19)
  • Travel/ODC estimates will be included in Section B of final SIR (Q&A #65)
  • Escalation rates are offeror-defined — inflation formulas added to pricing workbook (Q&A #64, #67)

LCATs — Extensive Revision Coming

  • J002 LCATs will be "extensively revised" to align with PEARS II categories (Q&A #39)
  • Education substitution policies being standardized across categories
  • GIS Specialist folded into "IT Analyst" category (Q&A #68)
  • All positions require relevant experience — no zero-experience positions (Q&A #13)

Technical Approach

  • 34-page tech proposal for 17 SOW elements — FAA "may revise" this limit (Q&A #37). That's ~2 pages per element — extremely tight
  • 17 elements rated as "equal importance" — industry pushed back; FAA response unclear on whether weighting will change
  • 4 senior staff resumes (3 pages each, outside page count) — positions TBD, will be specified in final SIR (Q&A #46, #47)
  • No contract-level Key Personnel — only designated at TO level (Q&A #46)
  • Security clearances removed from base requirement (Q&A #45)

Acquisition Strategy

  • Single-award IDIQ — not multi-award (implied by structure)
  • No phased downselect — FAA rejected industry recommendation twice (Q&A #16, #17)
  • Proposals submitted in PDF — format clarified (Q&A #6)
  • Transition from incumbent expected — FAA acknowledged this (Q&A implied)

Price Realism Risk

FAA reserves the right to perform price realism AND cost analysis (M.4(d)). Industry asked for "unrealistically low price" rejection language — FAA declined. This means aggressive pricing won't be auto-rejected, but evaluators can adjust your proposed costs upward if they think your rates are too low to perform.

VII. Strategic Assessment for Changeis

Fit Analysis & Positioning

Moderate Fit — Niche Entry Possible via Teaming

The Opportunity

  • Total Small Business Set-Aside — large firms can't prime. Opens the field for smaller, specialized teams
  • $47M size standard — generous for small business. Changeis qualifies
  • Policy analysis and program management components — C.3.1 through C.3.17 include regulatory analysis, policy research, economic analysis, training, and technical writing — all Changeis core competencies
  • T&M task orders — rate-based billing, manageable risk profile
  • DC-based — aligns with Changeis geographic presence
The Challenge

Deep Aviation Environmental Expertise Required

  • This is a specialized environmental engineering contract — NEPA, AEDT, noise modeling, air quality, emissions, climate science. The core work requires aviation environmental SMEs with deep modeling experience
  • Past performance: 3 federal examples, 36-month recency — Changeis would need relevant environmental/engineering contract performance. ATAPS-II is human capital/IT, not environmental engineering
  • NAICS 541330 (Engineering Services) — not professional services or consulting. The FAA is buying engineering expertise
  • No phased downselect — full proposal required upfront, no advisory elimination rounds

Recommended Approach

Option: Sub to an Environmental Engineering Small Business
  • Target role: Subcontractor for program management, policy analysis, organizational/analytical support, and potentially data analytics/AI augmentation
  • Ideal prime partners: JD Rovolus (current incumbent), ATAC (former incumbent), or another small business with AEDT/environmental modeling credentials
  • Changeis value-add: Program management rigor, analytical methodology, organizational effectiveness, and potential agentic services for automating environmental data processing and reporting
  • Agentic angle: Environmental impact analysis involves massive data processing (noise contours, air quality modeling, emissions calculations). Agent-augmented delivery could differentiate on efficiency in FFP task orders
  • Watch for the final SIR — the revised LCATs and SOW will clarify exactly which roles align with Changeis capabilities
Bottom line: PEARS III is a specialized aviation environmental contract. Changeis should not prime this — the past performance and technical requirements are too deep in environmental engineering. But there's a legitimate subcontractor play on the policy, analytical, program management, and data/AI dimensions. Worth tracking through final SIR. Add to XSAM watchlist. — VM2 Strategic Assessment, March 11, 2026
