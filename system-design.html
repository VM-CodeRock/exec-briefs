01. The Thesis

The federal government is at an inflection point. AI is moving from pilots to operations, but the gap between knowing it matters and actually executing is enormous. 85% of leaders say adaptability is critical. 7% are doing it.

That gap is where thought leaders — and consulting firms — create value. The system described here is designed to:

  • Learn faster than peers by processing more signal with less noise
  • Synthesize raw intelligence into original points of view
  • Publish consistently across formats that reach different audiences
  • Compound authority over time through a content flywheel that builds on itself

This isn't a content marketing strategy. It's an intelligence operation that produces thought leadership as a byproduct.

04. Layer 2 — Analysis & Synthesis

Raw intelligence is worthless without interpretation. Every piece that enters the system goes through a structured analysis:

1
Signal Detection

Is this new? Does it affect federal transformation, AI adoption, human capital, or GovCon strategy? If not, discard.

2
Deep Research

Pull the source document. Read the actual report, not just the headline. Cross-reference with 2-3 additional sources. Quantify where possible.

3
Structured Brief Section

Write in the VEB format: context → key findings (with metrics) → detail bullets → source quote → Strategic Implication that ties to federal transformation.

4
Connection Mapping

How does this connect to other signals? What pattern is emerging? What should a federal leader do about it? This is where original POV is created.

The VEB Section Pattern: Numbered (I, II, III...) with category label. Context paragraph → metrics bar → detail bullets → blockquote from source → bold "Strategic Implication" paragraph. Newest sections first. Every section ends with "so what" for a federal leader.

06. Layer 4 — Distribution & Broadcasting

Content goes to different channels with different voices and formats:

Personal Brand (Your Name)

POV, opinions, commentary, "here's what I think" pieces. This is where thought leadership lives.

  • LinkedIn posts — 2-3x/week. Stat card + 3-paragraph commentary. Personal voice.
  • Transformation Signal newsletter — Weekly Friday digest. 3-5 curated insights + your POV. Substack or LinkedIn Newsletter.
  • Video briefs — Your avatar presenting key findings. LinkedIn native video.
  • Conference talks / webinars — Quarterly. Built from accumulated briefs and synthesis.

Company Brand (Changeis)

Wins, capabilities, hiring, thought leadership that positions the firm. Professional, institutional voice.

  • LinkedIn company page — 1-2x/week. Award wins, hiring posts, client success framing.
  • Internal staff emails — Monthly all-hands, referral programs, culture updates.
  • Client-facing briefs — Curated subset of VEB relevant to specific contracts (FAA, DHS, FTA).
  • Proposal inputs — VEB sections repurposed as "market awareness" evidence in bids.

09. Data Architecture

The system's data layer evolves with scale:

Now
JSON on GitHub Pages

data/briefs.json — 10 entries and growing. Lightweight, version-controlled, zero infrastructure. Supports filtering, search, and feed rendering.

200+
SQLite on VPS

When entries hit 200-300, migrate to SQLite for full-text search, relational queries, analytics. JSON export for Git Pages rendering. API endpoint for integrations.

Team
Supabase (Postgres)

When the team needs access: real-time collaboration, row-level security, REST API, dashboard for non-technical users. Changeis analysts can query and contribute.

Data Schema

briefs.json

Daily entries: id, date, title, summary, categories, section, source URL, metrics, brief URL

comms.json

Internal comms pipeline: id, date, title, type, status (idea→drafting→review→ready→sent), draft URL

sources.json

Feed registry: name, URL, category, check frequency, last checked timestamp

content-calendar.json

Publishing schedule: week, day, channel (personal/company), content type, status

11. Positioning Strategy

The intelligence system serves a deliberate positioning arc:

Phase 1: Establish Cadence (Weeks 1-4)

Daily VEB briefs. 2-3 LinkedIn posts per week. Prove consistency and quality. Build the habit. Current phase.

Phase 2: Build Audience (Months 2-3)

Launch Transformation Signal newsletter. Video briefs with personal avatar. Engage with federal CIO/CTO/CHRO community on LinkedIn. Start commenting on others' posts with VEB-informed insights.

Phase 3: Claim the Lane (Months 4-6)

Monthly long-form articles. First conference submission (ACT-IAC, PSC, ATARC). Guest appearances on federal tech podcasts. "The AI-Driven Federal Transformation" becomes your recognized lane.

Phase 4: Compound Authority (Months 6-12)

Speaking invitations. Panel moderator roles. Media quotes. Inbound partnership requests. The VEB archive becomes a searchable knowledge base that no competitor can replicate — because they didn't start 6 months ago.

The Moat: Consistency + compounding. Anyone can write one good LinkedIn post. Almost no one publishes a researched daily intelligence brief with audio, PDF, and graphics, cross-referenced with strategic implications for federal leaders. By month 6, the archive alone is a competitive asset.
