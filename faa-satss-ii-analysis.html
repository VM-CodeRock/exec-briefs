Executive Summary

This is the recompete of the AVS Safety, Analytical and Technical Support Services contract — the FAA's Aviation Safety (AVS) organization's primary analytical and technical support vehicle. It covers everything from program management and safety analysis to UAS integration, Data Comm, Performance-Based Navigation, weather programs, and flight simulation. The contract is an IDIQ with FFP and T&M task orders, based at Washington, D.C. (all rates priced to DC), with some work at the Mike Monroney Aeronautical Center in Oklahoma City.

Scale: ~56 FTEs across 18 labor categories. At typical DC loaded rates ($150-250/hr), the 5-year estimated value is in the $80-140M range. The current incumbent is likely LS Technologies or one of the firms at the pre-proposal conference. 13 companies attended the pre-proposal conference, including BCG, SAIC, Noblis, CSSI, MZI Aviation, and others.

Key signals: The CO has mandated ~90% FFP task orders (per CFO directive to eliminate T&M). Amendment 0001 removed the single-compensation-plan requirement for teams, making teaming much easier. Past performance is evaluated for both prime and sub work. Proposals are due March 23, 2026 — 12 days away.

IV. Evaluation Criteria

How They'll Pick the Winner

Best Value, Full Trade-Off — technical excellence can outweigh price. This is NOT lowest-price-technically-acceptable.

Factor 1 — Technical (Most Important)

Four Sub-Factors

  • 1.1 Technical Approach / Program Management / Supervision Plan — How you'll manage 56 FTEs, supervise task orders, coordinate teaming partners, and deliver across 12 task areas
  • 1.2 Knowledge & Expertise — Demonstrated capability across ALL 12 task areas (aviation safety, UAS, PBN, Data Comm, weather, SMS, flight simulation, etc.)
  • 1.3 Key Personnel / Resumes — Sr. Program Manager (1 resume) + Project Manager (1 resume). 3 pages each, excluded from 75-page limit. Education, credentials, supervisory experience
  • 1.4 Quality Control Plan — Inspection system covering all 12 task areas
Factor 2 — Past Performance (Second Most Important)

2-4 Contracts, Plus PPS Surveys

  • Minimum 2, maximum 4 contracts demonstrating similar Safety, Analytical and Technical Support Services
  • Must be within last 5 years through closing date
  • Max 1 in-progress contract (with at least 1 year completed)
  • Subs can submit subcontract references if insufficient prime contracts
  • PPS (Past Performance Survey, Attachment 5) must be completed by each cited client — more detailed than CPARS
  • Contract value and number of employees are part of relevancy evaluation (Section M.3)
  • CPARS are NOT accepted in lieu of PPS
Factor 3 — Price (Least Important)

Total Evaluated Price Across All 5 Years

  • Composite hourly rates × estimated hours per labor category per year
  • All rates based on Washington, D.C. location
  • Must provide estimating rationale: Direct Labor, Overhead, ODC, G&A, Profit traceable to salary survey data
  • No certified cost data required
  • Labor escalation methodology at offeror's discretion (exempt from SCA)

VI. Key Takeaways from Q&A and Conference

What the CO Revealed

Major Changes from Amendment 0001

Several offeror-favorable revisions

  • Single compensation plan REMOVED (Q13) — Prime and subs no longer need unified pay/benefits. Makes teaming dramatically easier
  • Key personnel resumes EXCLUDED from 75-page limit (Q4) — 3 pages each, separately counted
  • Teaming agreements UNLIMITED pages (Q20) — No longer capped at 10 pages per TA
  • NAICS corrected to 541990 (Q1/Q47) — Professional/Scientific/Technical services, not Security Guards
  • Aviation insurance (H.7) REMOVED (Q26)
  • Sub past performance accepted (Q11/Q45) — Can cite subcontract work with government PPS reviewer
Critical Intelligence from Q&A

What the CO told the market

  • ~90% FFP task orders (Q56) — CFO mandate to eliminate T&M. This is a fundamental shift — offerors who can't price FFP task orders competitively will lose
  • All rates based on D.C. (Q7/Q25/Q28) — No regional rate variations
  • Fully loaded rates at award apply to all task orders (Q57) — No renegotiation at task order level. Must price escalation up front
  • No SCA coverage (Q59) — Labor categories are exempt. No annual equitable adjustments. Offerors absorb wage escalation risk
  • No certified cost data required (Q60) — But must show traceable Direct Labor → Overhead → G&A → Profit for each LCAT
  • Model contract required (Q33) — Full Sections A-J in Volume I, even untouched sections
  • The CO has a sense of humor (Q18: "The Contracting Officer was sleep-deprived"; Q31: "Don't forget to spring forward"; Q46: "Welcome to the wonderful world of PRISM")

Realistic Assessment

Where Does Changeis Play?

This is a large, highly specialized aviation safety support contract. The technical depth required across 12 task areas — many deeply aviation-specific — makes this a difficult prime opportunity without existing aviation safety past performance at scale.

Most viable paths:

  • Subcontractor to a prime — Offer program management, analytical support, SMS, and HC/organizational capabilities under a prime like CSSI, Metis, or Noblis
  • Teaming partner providing 5-10 FTEs — Program management analysts, communication specialists, regulatory analysts where Changeis has clear credentials
  • Strategic intelligence — Even if not bidding, understanding this contract informs ATAPS-II positioning and future AVS opportunities
VIII. Agentic Services Angle

Connection to the Cost-Share Model

This contract is an interesting test case for the agentic services model we're developing:

  • 90% FFP task orders — Perfect for agent-augmented delivery. FFP rewards efficiency; if agents can reduce the hours needed to complete a fixed-price deliverable, the margin goes straight to the contractor
  • Analytical support tasks — Tasks 3.1.2 (program analysis) and 3.1.3 (technical analysis) include data analysis, reporting, and policy review that agents can accelerate
  • Communication specialist — Briefing prep, stakeholder updates, meeting summaries — high agent automation potential
  • Technical writing — 2.7 FTEs of technical writers could be augmented significantly with AI

Not viable for this bid (due in 12 days), but if Changeis participates as a sub, it could propose agent-augmented delivery for the analytical and communications tasks — demonstrating the model on a real contract.
