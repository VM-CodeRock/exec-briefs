Executive Summary

The FAA is conducting fact-finding for a massive cryptographic transformation — migrating both its safety-critical National Airspace System (NAS) and enterprise business IT to post-quantum cryptography. This covers everything: ERAM (enroute ATC), STARS (terminal ATC), ADS-B, Data Comm, SWIM, FENS/BNATCS, weather systems, NOTAMs, and all enterprise IT. The eventual contract will likely be multi-billion dollar, touching every system the FAA operates. Peraton is the BNATCS prime integrator, but PQC is being sourced separately.

Why Changeis should respond: The FAA is explicitly open to both an Integrated (Model A) and Modular (Model B) approach. They acknowledge no single vendor can do this alone. This creates space for specialized consulting firms with FAA institutional knowledge to participate — either as teaming partners, integration advisors, or specialist subcontractors. Changeis's existing ATAPS-II relationship, FAA domain expertise, and IT modernization credentials position it well for the advisory and workforce transformation aspects of this transition.

II. Scope of PQC Migration

Two Domains: NAS (Operational Technology) + Enterprise IT

The FAA explicitly divides the PQC challenge into two domains, each with fundamentally different requirements:

Domain 1 — NAS / Operational Technology (Safety-Critical)

National Airspace System — Air Traffic Control Infrastructure

The most complex ATC system in the world: 45,000+ flights daily, 29 million square miles, 400+ radar sites, 40,000 ground radios, 20 ARTCCs, hundreds of TRACONs and towers.

  • ERAM — Enroute Automation Modernization (high-altitude ATC for 20 centers)
  • STARS — Standard Terminal Automation Replacement System (approach/departure ATC)
  • ADS-B — Satellite-based surveillance, currently uses classical authentication. PQC must migrate ADS-B-AUTH to counter quantum-enabled spoofing
  • Data Comm / CPDLC — Digital controller-pilot messaging, relies on classical crypto for authenticity
  • SWIM — System Wide Information Management, the data-sharing backbone using PKI
  • WAAS — GPS augmentation for precision approaches, secured by classical digital signatures
  • ASDE-X / ASSC — Airport surface detection and runway safety
  • FENS / BNATCS — Next-gen network backbone replacing FTI; Peraton is prime integrator
  • ASOS / AWOS — Automated weather observation systems
  • NOTAMs / NMS — Notice to Airmen modernization (moving to cloud-based)

Key constraints: Sub-millisecond latency requirements, DO-178C/DO-254 certification, safety-critical reliability, 24/7/365 uptime, remote site deployment, embedded hardware platforms.

Domain 2 — Enterprise IT (Administrative)

FAA Business Systems — Office of Information & Technology Services (AIT)

  • HR and personnel records (decades-long confidentiality lifespan)
  • Financial systems and procurement
  • Cloud and SaaS platforms
  • IAM / Zero Trust Network Architecture (ZTNA)
  • VPN, IPsec/TLS tunnels for administrative traffic
  • Third-party integrations and partner networks

Key concern: "Harvest Now, Decrypt Later" (HNDL) — adversaries collecting encrypted data today to decrypt with future quantum computers. HR/financial records with 20+ year confidentiality requirements are especially vulnerable.

III. Procurement Models Under Consideration

Model A (Integrated) vs. Model B (Modular)

The FAA has not committed to a procurement model — this RFI is explicitly evaluating both approaches:

AspectModel A: One-Stop ShopModel B: Modular/Component
ConceptSingle lead integrator provides comprehensive PQC suite (algorithms, key management, hardware)Government sources individual subsystems from specialized providers
ProsSpeed of deployment, simplified compliance auditing, single accountabilityBest-of-breed components, crypto-agility, reduced vendor lock-in
RisksVendor lock-in, single point of failure, may not cover both IT and OTMulti-vendor interoperability challenges, integration complexity
Cost FactorLower integration labor, higher licensing"Integration tax" for cross-vendor interop
FAA Acknowledgment"A single provider may not have validated all necessary cryptographic primitives across all hardware and software layers" — they know no one can do it all alone

V. Vendor Questions — What They're Asking (7 Categories)

The 30-Page Response Must Address These

Each category has Shared (Common Core), NAS-Specific (Safety-Critical/OT), and Enterprise IT (Administrative) sub-questions. Here's what they want:

3.1 — Technology Tiers Supported

Which tiers does your product cover? Model A or B?

Also: IT-only, OT-only, or integrated IT+OT capabilities? Each requires different framing in the response.

3.2 — PQC Algorithm & Implementation

Crypto Specifics

  • Algorithm Spec: Which NIST PQC algorithms? Security levels? Library versions?
  • Cryptographic Agility: How to swap algorithms without new hardware baseline?
  • FIPS 140-3 Validation: Evidence or timeline for validation
  • Seed Protection: Side-channel leakage defense during key derivation
  • NAS: Integration with NAS Enterprise HSMs, assembly-level optimizations for real-time
  • IT: Integration with Enterprise Key Management Infrastructure (EKMI)
3.3 — Interoperability & System Integration

The Hardest Section — 8 Sub-Questions

  • Protocol Engineering: TLS 1.3/IKEv2 hybrid key exchanges, deterministic fallback
  • Connectivity: IP fragmentation prevention, Certificate Compression (RFC 8879)
  • Pluggable Crypto: Support for sovereign algorithm alternatives without full rebuild
  • Asset Inventory: Methodology for comprehensive cryptographic asset inventory across all FAA systems (COTS, custom, SaaS)
  • Milestone Roadmap: Aligned with FAA's 5-phase AMS process; must identify systems unable to support PQC
  • HNDL Re-encryption: Strategy for re-encrypting archived data with >5-year confidentiality lifespan
  • Post-Migration Audit: Tools, metrics, procedures for monitoring deployed PQC infrastructure
  • Deployment Plan: Phased rollout strategy to minimize operational disruption
  • NAS-Specific: ATN/IPS support, legacy OT isolation (crypto gateways), ICAO/Eurocontrol compliance
  • IT-Specific: IAM/ZTNA migration to PQC, external PQC enforcement proxies, legacy IT isolation
3.4 — Performance & Real-Time Impact

Can PQC Run at ATC Speed?

  • Resource Consumption: Peak CPU/memory under high load, compliance with FAA utilization ceilings
  • PQC Failover: Safe return to classical mode if PQC component degrades
  • NAS: Mean/95th percentile latency on embedded platforms; message size overhead for control messages
  • IT: Performance on high-volume admin traffic; HNDL prioritization by data confidentiality lifespan
3.5 — Software & Supply Chain Risk Management

Security of the Security Layer

  • Side-channel resistance and timing attack defenses
  • Rapid patching plan for newly discovered PQC vulnerabilities
  • Root of Trust migration to PQC digital signatures (offline HSM protection)
  • Secure provisioning of embedded chips and FPGAs (backdoor-free)
  • PQC partner ecosystem for multi-vendor COTS/SaaS interoperability
3.6 — Costs (ROM)

Rough Order of Magnitude Pricing

  • Cost Categories: NRE, Hardware Acquisition, Licensing/Software, Implementation/Deployment
  • TCO Drivers: Model A integration savings vs. Model B "integration tax"; crypto-agility premium; parallel hybrid-mode costs
  • Scaling Metrics: Primary cost driver (endpoints? traffic volume? number of keys?)
  • Testing Resources: Non-Operational Test Environment requirements
  • Training: PQC training for operators, technicians, and cybersecurity analysts
  • NAS: Custom NAS integration costs (ERAM, DataComm), DAL certification costs
  • IT: Enterprise licensing, SaaS integration, archive re-encryption costs
3.7 — Vendor Insights

Strategic and Experience Questions

  • Inter-vendor Coordination: Non-technical challenges in coordinating PQC across multiple NAS vendors
  • Certification Challenges: DAL, DO-178C, DO-254 barriers for safety-critical PQC
  • Quantum Resistance: Defenses against QRAM attacks and future quantum advances
  • Joint Acceleration: Where FAA and industry can work together to speed PQC readiness
  • NAS/OT Experience: Prior PQC integration in safety-critical OT environments
  • IT Enterprise Experience: Prior PQC in large-scale admin IT systems
